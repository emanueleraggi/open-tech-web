A Resume Landing Pages Showcase Study For Introducing Yourself Part 1

May 17th, 2020

After virtually walking a in Newport, RI, in our previous article, and sharing some advice for small business to get noticed on Google Suite Business, it is time now to explain in brief how the Search Engine Optimization works. First, what does SEO mean? Basically means being searchable or in another way increase as much as possible the number of visitors, and hopefully prospective customers, to your website. A golden standard to increase traffic would be comprehending social media in your page, in a way you are trying to market yourself so what would be the best way to do that? Well, first including keywords related to the webpage, in our case we are providing some suggestions of a resume therefore if you are a software engineer you may want to include keywords such as “C++” for low level application languages able to talk to sensors or “JavaScript” if you are mainly a web developer. “FPGA” if you are an embedded software engineer and so on. You will find incredible the amount of recruiters that will contact you on LinkedIn if you modify your profile adding those little and simple search words. So why not do the same with your business. Adding your resume on your webpage will certainly increase the chances of customer searching for you. Word of mouth is very powerful but social media plays their big role in marketing yourself in a very elegant but inexpensive way. In this proposal I used HTML, CSS and JavaScript, which is a powerful combination of programminhg language for designing a frontend user interface. In another example we will see the use of the Gatsby framework built on top of React that helps web applications develop at a higher pace, powered by the powerful QGraphQL, which is a query framework that allows to operate static and dynamic queries in a very special and specific framework. Witout further digression let's jump into the present case study. In the below resume page it is possible to see how the webpage is divided in different pages, each page has a special characteristic and the resume can be read online with information available on the spot. What type of suggestions stand out in comparison to other websites? Well, simplcity is the king! You name it. HTML, CSS and JavaScript are the languages to quickly design, develop and deploy a quick and simple solution for a final user. In addition to that it is shown the responsiveness of the website in the image below. As the user zoom in or zxoom out the page or uses its fingers the page is clearly responsive for the best user experience. Note below the navbar appearing only when the user click on the hand icon on thp right of the application. The appearance is simple and clearly communicates the professionality of the person:

In addition, another elegant way to talk about themselves would be to include some sort of articles, blog type, in your resume and create some contents. Content creation plays a vital role in web development as also shown through the Google Trends Search capability. As it is possible to see in the past 5 years, businesses, whether small or big, have been focusing their effort on the magic word “content creation”. Which is also why it is important to point out when somebody is trying to offer or sell a service.

What do you offer? Why is it better than your competitors and what are the different features that stand out? More on content creation and how it is important to create to stand out will be discussed in the next episode. So stay tuned and cone your skills.

How Bayes Concepts Can Be Applied For Better Results In The Healthcare Field

December 13th, 2020

Automating processes are becoming even more important in the healthcare industry as artificial intelligence and neural networks have opened up possibilities to help doctors in quicker and shared decisions. Artificial intelligence has already made its way in several applications such as helping in classifying between patients with Parkinson’s disease and healthy (W. Zeng, C. Yuan, Q. Wang et al., 2019), or by finding the epileptogenic focus for medical treatment in the case of a partial epilepsy by automatically detecting its electroencephalogram signals (W. Zeng et al., 2019). An additional example is given by the possibility of tracking cells’ trajectory over time, or lineage, as they move (Juang et al., 2009). The aforementioned are just a few of the implementations we can see in the new digital era and new methodologies are currently underway such as how the Brain Machine Interface interacts with a completely motionless patient, or how a BMI interacts with down syndrome patients.

There are two interesting projects underway such as the BCI2000 which is a general-purpose system for basic and clinical neurophysiological research and the OPenBCI project. Assistive robotic systems operated via a Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) are a reality and complex systems are under development in combination with the Robotic Operating System ROS. One in 4 U.S. adults – 61 million Americans – have a disability that impacts major life activities.

The most common disability type, declining mobility, affects 1 in 7 adults. With age, disability becomes more common, affecting about 2 in 5 adults ages 65 and older. For many people with mobility issues, essential and simple tasks, such as dressing or feeding, require additional help. Thus, the use of devices providing independent mobility can have a significant impact on their quality of life and daily operations. Another factor is cost too. With age, continuous help could be needed daily, increasing personal cost and that is a problem if additional years towards retirement are needed. Therefore giving back some sort of independence can save money and also can support quality of life and longevity.

As pointed out by the CDC Division of Human Development and Disability,

People with disabilities will benefit from care coordination and better access to health care and the health services they need, so that they adopt healthy behaviors and have better health -- CDC Division of Human Development and Disability

Research showing how many people have a disability and differences in their access to health care can guide efforts by health care providers and public health practitioners to improve access to care for people with disabilities -- CDC Division of Human Development and Disability

Additional studies based on Bayes principles helped in predicting patients' response to treatments. Patients’ health status and the likelihood of treatment options for helping doctors and patients to monitor progressions of diseases for future clinical decision. In the drug development environment there was evidence that a Bayes approach can provide an efficient method for evaluating new molecules during the early phases of drug development.

In addition to what was mentioned so far, a comprehensive literature review was also conducted to support Bayesian networks for use during clinical decision making and evidence suggests that Bayesian models could be extremely useful in the healthcare field.

Research is underway in this field but it is possible to state that Bayes theory is and will play a significant role in the predition of some dideases and response to treatments, if this technique is associated with machine learning and artifical intelligence great predicting platform can be created for everyone in need.
